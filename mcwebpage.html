What is Minecraft?

Minecraft is a hit game known for being composed entirely by cubes. Even the items have this pixelated look. It was launched in 2011 by Mojang Studios, its main creator being known as Notch. Ever since then, it became one of the best selling videogames of all time.

Where can you play?

Minecraft is available in the following platforms and formats:

  • Java Edition (PCs)
  • Consoles:Play Stations, X box, Nintendo Switch
  • iOS and Android (Pocket Edition)

How to play Minecraft

Basics: Objectives and Game Mechanics

Minecraft can be played in 3 modes, Survival, Creative, and Adventure. There are also servers available for multiplayer games, but now we're going to focus on the worlds you can create yourself. The difference in the modes are that in survival, you objective is to stay alive and kill the Enderdragon, you have a hunger bar and a life bar and you can freely interact with the world. Adventure mode is very similar, bur your interaction with the world is similar. In creative mode, you have access to all of the blocks without having to seek resources, and you are immortal. Here, we are going to focus on how to play on survival mode.

Movement & Controls

On a mobile device, you can move by clicking the arrows that appear on the screen. On a keyboard (set to default), you use the WASD keys for moving and the space bar to jump, the E key to open the inventory, shift to Crouch and you can sprint by hittimng the W key twice. Then to destroy blocks you would use the left click and to place them the right click. All of this can be changed in the settings. In any other console or device, check the setting for your controllers.

Blocks and tools

The Minecraft world is completely consisted of blocks. You'll probably see trees or dirt blocks first, but you can also mine stone and pretty much any other block in the game. However, some may need tools to be acquired. To craft those tools, you're going to need a crafting table, which is what you use to combine you collected resources into items. Other blocks you may use a lot are the furnace, which you can smelt ores and cook food, and the chest, which you may use for storage. There are other various blocks with functions, and you may discover how to use them as you play the game. Those include enchanting tables, anvils, cartography tables, and much more.
Now to acquire those blocks and actually be able to function in game, you need tools. The ones you will use the most are the Pickaxe, Axe, and Sword. There are other, like the shovel, but they're not as common to use and are pretty self explanatory.

Tool Material Function
Pickaxe Wood, Stone, Iron, Gold, Diamond, and Netherite. Used for mining anything from stone to obsidian.
Axe Wood, Stone, Iron, Gold, Diamond, and Netherite. Used for collecting wood and self defense since it inflicts a lot of damage
Sword Wood, Stone, Iron, Gold, Diamond, and Netherite. Used for battling/ self-defense

Mobs

There are two types of mobs in minecraft, hostile and passive. The passive mobs, like cows and sheep won't attempt hurting you at all. But hostile mobs, like Zombies, Skeletons, Creepers (which you can see in the image aside) and many others, spawn in the night and live in the dark, with the objective of scaring you as a trap. You can fight them off with your weapons, or try running away. Be careful though, some have special abilities! Endermen for example, can teleport! Watch out for monsters, but you don't need to be afraid of approaching regular animals, or villagers, who resemble people(you can trade with them!).

Biomes and Dimensions

In Minecraft, you can find inumerous biomes, such as Grasslands, Ice Spikes, Forest, Desert, Savana, Swamps, Taigas, Mesas and many more! In minecraft, there is also more than one dimension. The world with all of those biomes is the overworld. Besides that, you can find the Nether and the End, where the ender dragon is located. To enter those, you need to make their specifc portal.
